Seeing Your Way to learning. How to Use Visioning to Create Tomorrow

Description

Visioning is oftentimes used as the first step in a health or financial wellness plan. What comes to mind when you ask yourself:

  • What do I most want to achieve?
  • What are my hopes for the future?
  • How do I see myself spending my time?
  • Where are the places I feel out-of-balance?

Exploring open-ended questions is just one of the practice opportunities you’ll find as you begin crafting your vision for personal well-being.

In this short (approximately 20 minute) interactive course, you’ll find multiple avenues for beginning your visioning process, including audio meditations and visualization exercises. This is not daydreaming; you’ll focus on what means the most to you in your life and how you envision your tomorrows. You can hone the visioning skills you already have, or learn from scratch about how visioning can help you move forward.


Duration
Approximately 20 minutes

User Guide:

Audio / Video
This course features video segments with audio narration. Turn your audio ON. Earphones or a headset may be necessary, depending on your viewing situation.

Navigation
This course was designed for viewing sequentially by clicking the NEXT button on the bottom right side of the viewer. However, the menu on the left-hand side is available for self-selecting content.

Personal Input
Throughout the course, you will find self-assessments and text fields that ask for your personal input. By entering relevant information, you will be better able to engage in the visioning process. Most of the interactions are optional; however, a few require your input. On these few slides, the NEXT button is not visible until you make a selection.

Your Information
Your personal input, whether answers to assessments/quizzes or text field entries, is NOT stored. It is for your use during an individual session, and will need to be re-entered each time you re-launch the course. You will be able to print the screen containing your Visioning process at the end of the course.

Links to External Content
To ensure that links to content outside the course open correctly, change your browser preferences to “open links in a new window.”
